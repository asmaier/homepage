It’s a shame that Sornette and co don’t back up their claims with economic theory, it would be interesting to see that.

I would add a bet that a basic multifractal process generates those kinds of fluctuations, on different scales. They never provide a theory to fully justify how the patterns identified would be special marking a bubble, and how these same patterns could be captured within a bottom-up fashion. Furthermore, if they identity multifractal scaling in the markets how do they reconcile that with this special scale specific phenomenon, doesn’t that break the multifractality? And if it breaks, why does it break, what are the economic reasons, for this?
